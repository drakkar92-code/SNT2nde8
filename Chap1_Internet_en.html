Internet

Internet is a global computer network of interconnected computers that enables the exchange of data and resources using standardized protocols such as TCP/IP. It consists of a network of millions of both public and private, academic, commercial, and governmental networks. These are grouped into autonomous networks. Today, there are more than 91,000 distinct networks.

  • Internet is a worldwide network that connects billions of digital devices, such as computers, smartphones, and servers, enabling fast and reliable information exchange. Operating through standardized protocols like TCP/IP, the Internet facilitates communication between users, access to vast amounts of data, and the use of various services, such as web browsing, email, streaming, and social media. It is structured around a vast physical infrastructure, including submarine cables, satellites, and data centers, ensuring data transmission across the globe. As an essential tool of modern society, the Internet transforms human interactions, commerce, education, and many other fields while also raising challenges related to cybersecurity, regulation, and digital inclusion.
